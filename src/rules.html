Rules of Checkers

Win Conditions

  • Your opponent has no pieces left.
  • Your opponent can no longer move.

Legal Moves

  • You may only move one piece per turn.
  • At the start of the game, Pieces can only move forward. They may move forward and diaganally one square.
  • If an opponents piece is blocking your piece, you may jump over it and take their piece, so long as there is an empty space directly behind the opponents piece along the same diaganal.
  • If it is possible to take a piece, you must take it. If more than one piece has the opportunity to take an opposing piece, you can choose which one you move.
  • If, after taking an enemies piece, there is a chance to take another piece with the same piece, you must also take it. This repeats until it is impossible to take any more pieces or the piece is kinged.

Crowning pieces

  • If your piece manages to make its way to the far end of the board, it is kinged by having another piece placed on top of it.
  • A piece may also become a king if it takes an opponents king.
  • A piece being kinged ends your turn, even if there are still pieces which can be taken.
  • Kings can move and take on any diaganal.
